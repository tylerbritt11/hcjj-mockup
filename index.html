Your First Class

What to expect

No experience or gear needed — here's how your free first class goes.

Step 01

Come as you are

Wear light athletic clothing and arrive a few minutes early to sign a waiver. A clean loaner gi will be ready for you.

Step 02

Learn the room

Meet your coach and your training partners, and get walked through the movement fundamentals at a beginner's pace.

Step 03

Train at your pace

Drill, ask questions, and if you'd rather watch the live rounds than roll on day one — that's fine.

Common questions

Is jiu-jitsu for me?

Yes. We have students of all ages, shapes, and sizes — from couch potatoes to seasoned athletes. The first class is free, no strings attached.

What should I wear?

Light, comfortable clothing. We'll loan you a gi for your first class, free of charge.

Do I need to sign a contract?

No. Month-to-month membership is the standard, and quarterly billing is an optional discount — we want you training because you like to, not because you have to.

Will I get hurt?

Safety is the first job of every class. Beginners are guided at their own pace, classes are capped at 15 adults, and you never have to spar before you're ready.

What age can my kid start?

Our kids program is for ages 8–14, Tuesdays and Thursdays at 5:00 PM. Their first class is free too, and parents can watch from the seating area.

What's the difference between gi and no-gi?

Gi classes use the traditional uniform and build technique through grips and control. No-gi is faster-paced, in a rash guard and shorts. We recommend training both — and your membership covers both.

Is there somewhere to change and shower?

Yes — a changing room, shower, and bathroom are on site, plus spectator seating and plenty of parking in our lot and the adjacent hospital lot.
